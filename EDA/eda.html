items = []
for sp_dir in DATA_PATH.iterdir():
    if not sp_dir.is_dir(): 
        continue
    for ch_dir in sp_dir.iterdir():
        if not ch_dir.is_dir(): 
            continue
        trans = ch_dir / f"{sp_dir.name}-{ch_dir.name}.trans.txt"
        if not trans.exists(): 
            continue
        for flac in ch_dir.glob("*.flac"):
            items.append({
                "speaker": sp_dir.name,
                "chapter": ch_dir.name,
                "flac": flac,
                "trans": trans
            })

print(f"Found {len(items)} utterances across {len({i['speaker'] for i in items})} speakers.")

Found 2703 utterances across 40 speakers.

In[13]:
trans_cache = {}

for entry in items:
    tp = entry["trans"]
    if tp not in trans_cache:
        d = {}
        with open(tp, encoding="utf-8") as f:
            for line in f:
                line = line.strip()
                if not line or line.startswith(";"):
                    continue
                # split at first space only
                utt_id, text = line.split(" ", 1)
                d[utt_id] = text
        trans_cache[tp] = d

# Spot-check
some_tp = next(iter(trans_cache))
print(f"Loaded {len(trans_cache[some_tp])} utterances from {some_tp.name}")

records = []

for entry in items:
    utt_id = entry["flac"].stem
    
    # measure the audio duration in seconds
    dur_s = sf.info(entry["flac"]).duration
    
    # transcript text
    txt = trans_cache[entry["trans"]].get(utt_id, "")
    
    # count words
    n_words = len(txt.split())
    
    # record the row
    records.append({
        "speaker_id": entry["speaker"],
        "chapter_id": entry["chapter"],
        "utt_id":     utt_id,
        "duration_s": dur_s,
        "n_words":    n_words,
        "text":       txt
    })

From these 2,703 utterances in dev-clean, we see:

  • Duration

    • The average snippet is about 7.2 seconds long ( 4.7 s).
    • Half of all utterances fall between roughly 3.8 s (25th pct) and 9.3 s (75th pct), with a median of 5.9 s.
    • The shortest clip is about 1.45 s, and the longest is around 32.6 s, so there are a few much longer passages.

  • Word count

    • On average each utterance contains about 20 words ( 13.5).
    • The middle 50% spans from 10 words (25th pct) to 26 words (75th pct), with a median of 17 words.
    • We see utterances as short as a single word and as long as 92 words in the extreme tail.

Interpretation: most utterances are brief (around 6 s and 17 words), which is typical for clean read speech. The righthand tails (long clips >20 s or >50 words) represent outliers you may want to inspect or handle specially when building your ASR pipelines.
